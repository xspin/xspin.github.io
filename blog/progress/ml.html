The Problem of Overfiting

undefitting

  • Underfitting, or high bias, is when the form of our hypothesis function h maps poorly to the trend of the data. It is usually caused by a function that is too simple or uses too few features.

overfitting

  • Overfitting, or high variance, is caused by a hypothesis function that fits the available data but does not generalize well to predict new data. It is usually caused by a complicated function that creates a lot of unnecessary curves and angles unrelated to the data.

Two main options to address the issue

  • Reduce the number of features
    • Manually select which features to keep
    • Use a model selection algorithm
  • Regularization
    • Keep all the features, but reduce the magnitude of parameters
    • Regularization works well when we have a lot of slightly useful features

Regularized Linear Regression
$$
J(\theta)=\frac{1}{2m}\left[\sum_{i=1}^m(h_\theta(x^{(i)})-y^{(i)})^2+\lambda\sum_{j=1}^n\theta_j^2\right]
=\frac{1}{2m}[(y-X\theta)^T(y-X\theta)+\lambda|\theta’|] $$

$$
\theta=(X^TX+\lambda, \mathrm{diag}([0,1,1,…,1])^{-1}X^Ty
$$

Regularized Logistic Regression
$$
\begin{array}.
J(\theta)=-\frac{1}{m}\left[\sum_{i=1}^my^{(i)}\log h_\theta(x^{(i)})+(1-y^{(i)})\log(1-h_\theta(x^{(i)})\right] + \frac{\lambda}{2m}\sum_{j=1}^n\theta_j^2\
= -\frac{1}{m}\left[y^T\log h_\theta(X)+(1-y)^T\log_\theta(X)\right]+\frac{\lambda}{2m} \theta’^T\theta’
\end{array}
$$
where $\theta’=[\theta_1,\cdots,\theta_n]^T$.

Back Propagation

Neural Network

  • layer 1 (input): $A’_1=X$
  • layer 2: $Z_2=\Theta_1A_1, A’_2=g(Z_2)$
  • …
  • layer k: $Z_k=\Theta_{k-1}A_{k-1}, A’_k=g(Z_k)$
  • …
  • layer L (output): $Z_L=\Theta_{L-1}A_{L-1}, A’_L=g(Z_L)$

Error Function $J(\Theta)=|A’_L-Y|^2/2$

Partial Derivation on $\Theta_{L-1}$
$$
\begin{align}
\frac{\partial J}{\partial \Theta^{ij}{L-1}} & =\delta_L^T \cdot \frac{\partial A’_L}{\partial \Theta^{ij}{L-1}} \
& = \delta_L^T \cdot g’(Z_{L})\frac{\partial Z_L}{\partial \Theta^{ij}{L-1}} \
&= \delta_L^T \cdot g’(Z{L}) I_{L,j}(A_{L-1,j})
\end{align}
$$
where $\delta_L = A’L-Y$. Thus,
$$
\begin{align}
\frac{\partial J}{\partial \Theta^{i:}{L-1}}
&= Diag(A_{L-1}) \cdot \delta_Lg’(Z_{L}) = A_{L-1} \delta_Lg’(Z_{L})
\end{align}
$$
Partial Derivation on $\Theta_{k-1}$
$$
\begin{align}
\frac{\partial J}{\partial \Theta^{ij}{k-1}} & =\delta_L^T \cdot \frac{\partial A’_L}{\partial \Theta^{ij}{k-1}} \
& = \delta_L^T \cdot g’(Z_{L})\frac{\partial Z_L}{\partial \Theta^{ij}{k-1}} \
&= \delta_L^T \cdot g’(Z{L}) \frac{\partial Z_L}{\partial A_{L-1}} \cdot \frac{\partial A_{L-1}}{\partial \Theta^{ij}{k-1}} \
&= \delta_L^T \cdot g’(Z{L}) \Theta_{L-1} \cdot \frac{\partial A_{L-1}}{\partial \Theta^{ij}{k-1}} \
&= \delta_L^T \cdot g’(Z{L}) \Theta_{L-1} \cdot [0; g’(Z_{L-1})\frac{\partial Z_{L-1}}{\partial \Theta^{ij}{k-1}}]
\end{align}
$$
Hence,
$$
\begin{align}
\frac{\partial J}{\partial \Theta^{i:}{k-1}}
&= \delta_L^T \cdot g’(Z_{L}) \Theta_{L-1} \cdot [0; g’(Z_{L-1})\frac{\partial Z_{L-1}}{\partial \Theta^{i:}_{k-1}}]
\end{align}
$$
